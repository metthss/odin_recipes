Caponata di Melanzane

Caponata (Sicilian: capunata) is a Sicilian dish consisting of chopped fried aubergine and other vegetables, seasoned with olive oil, tomato sauce, celery, olives, and capers, in an agrodolce sauce.

Numerous local variants exist concerning the ingredients, by adding carrots, bell peppers, potatoes, pine nuts, and raisins. There is a Palermo version that adds octopus, and an aristocratic Sicilian recipe includes lobster and swordfish garnished with wild asparagus, grated dried tuna roe and shrimp.

Ingredients

  • Melanzane
  • Sale
  • Pepe
  • Olio
  • Aceto
  • Miele
  • Passata di Pomodoro
  • Uvetta Secca
  • Pinoli
  • Sedano
  • Cipolla

Steps

  1. Boil the Sedano, then add it to a pan with Oil and Cipolla.
  2. Meanwhile, fry the chopped Melanzane, and let them rest. Add Salt according to your taste.
  3. Add Uvetta Secca, Pinoli and Pepe to the pan. Mix everything together.
  4. Add the fried Melanzane to the pan, together with the Passata di Pomodoro, and let everything cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add Miele and Aceto, and let everything cook for 10 minutes.
  6. Enjoy!
